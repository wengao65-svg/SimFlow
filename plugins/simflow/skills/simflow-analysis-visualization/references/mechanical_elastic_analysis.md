# Mechanical And Elastic Analysis

Use this reference for stress-strain curves, elastic constants, deformation,
fracture, yield, moduli, pressure-volume response, and mechanical figures.

## Required Evidence

- source logs/tables/trajectories, stress convention, strain definition, units, cell vectors, and reference configuration.
- deformation protocol, strain rate or finite strain step, thermostat/barostat settings, boundary conditions, and relaxation criteria.
- force-field/DFT/MLP provenance and whether stresses are virial, Cauchy, engineering, or code-specific outputs.

## Methods

- Elastic constants: strain amplitudes, symmetry assumptions, fit window, stress/energy fit method, relaxation choices, and unit conversion.
- Stress-strain: smoothing/filtering, failure criterion, replicate/seeding, finite-size effects, and uncertainty.
- Equation of state or compression: volume grid, reference state, fit model, pressure/stress consistency, and residuals.

## Claim Limits

Mechanical claims are sensitive to boundary conditions, strain path, size, rate,
and stress definition. Record these before comparing to experiment or literature.
